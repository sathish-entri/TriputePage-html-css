APJ ABDUL KALAM

"Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: The People's President and India's Missile Man"

*Early Life and Education*: Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam came from a humble background and pursued a career in science and aerospace engineering.

*Scientific Contributions: Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in developing India's missile and space programs, earning him the title *Missile Man of India. His work in ISRO and DRDO led to significant advancements in India's defense technology.

*Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV)*: One of his greatest achievements was leading the development of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980, marking India's entry into space exploration.

*Missile Development*: Dr. Kalam was instrumental in the development of India's Agni and Prithvi missiles, strengthening the nation’s defense capabilities and making India self-reliant in missile techn

*Presidency (2002-2007): As the 11th President of India, Dr. Kalam was widely regarded as the *People’s President due to his close connection with the youth and his vision for a developed India by 2020.

*Visionary Leader and Educator*: Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam remained dedicated to inspiring and educating the youth of India. He believed in empowering the next generation through education and innovation.

*Legacy and Inspiration*: Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture to students. His legacy continues to inspire millions through his books, speeches, and the values of simplicity, hard work, and dreaming big. -->
